Do American voters think in ideological terms?

    The American Voter observed 4 categories of voters:
  • ideologues (ex: they vote Democratic because their political opinions are more liberal)
  • group benefits voters (ex: vote Democratic because "they seem to support working people, like me")
  • nature of the times voters (ex: vote Democratic because the economy was good when they were last in office)
  • no issue content (vote Democratic because they always have, or because they like the candidate's haircut)
